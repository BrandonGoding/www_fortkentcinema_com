Fort Kent Cinema's History.

For over a century, Fort Kent Cinema has been more than just a place to catch a movie. It's been a gathering spot, a memory maker, and a symbol of community pride here in Fort Kent, Maine. From its humble origins as an old barn turned public hall, where movies began to grace its screens as early as 1917, to its official establishment briefly as The Gem and then as The Savoy Theatre in 1918, this cherished venue has remained a constant presence in Fort Kent. Though the original Savoy Theatre stood where our parking lot is today, the heart of the Savoy lives on in the present day Century Theater which stands just a few feet away.

Fort Kent Cinema Mission

At Fort Kent Cinema, we are dedicated to enriching our community by providing a unique and accessible cinematic experience. We strive to entertain, educate, and inspire audiences of all ages through diverse film offerings that celebrate our local heritage and foster a deeper understanding of the world. As stewards of a long-standing community tradition, we commit to maintaining an inclusive, welcoming environment that brings people together through the power of film.

Fort Kent Cinema Vision

Our vision is to be a cultural hub in Fort Kent, sustaining the historical legacy of local cinema while evolving to meet contemporary needs. We aim to expand our influence as a center for film appreciation and learning, where the power of movies acts as a catalyst for community engagement and personal growth. Looking ahead, we will innovate and adapt, ensuring that our community not only preserves its cinematic heritage but also embraces the future through expanded programming and educational opportunities.
